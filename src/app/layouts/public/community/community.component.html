PGP Key for Bounty Submissions

The bounty submission form utilizes HTTPS to encrypt your submission in transit to the bug bounty team. We have confidence that this provides the confidentiality required for vulnerability submissions. However, if you want to send us a vulnerability using PGP we still want you to have this option.

Please realize that using PGP will likely slow down our response to your submission. Under normal circumstances, a developer can view security reports and respond immediately, i.e. get a patch going within minutes and even paging people out of bed if itâ€™s critical. If you PGP encrypt your message, only core members of the security team can triage your submission. Please be aware that if we receive an encrypted submission, unless your submission has an significant impact outside of GitHub, we will likely not reply utilizing PGP.
